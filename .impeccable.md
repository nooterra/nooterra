# Nooterra Design System

## Design Context

### Users
Founders and operators of small-to-mid-market businesses ($1M-$20M revenue) who are technical enough to understand systems but too busy to operate every detail manually. They're delegating real authority to AI agents — sending emails to customers, collecting invoices, managing schedules. They need to GOVERN, not OPERATE. Their context: checking the system 2-3 times per day, making policy decisions, reviewing escrowed actions, and monitoring that autonomous operations are within bounds.

### Brand Personality
**Elegant. Autonomous. Trusted.**

Voice: Confident, precise, never salesy. Every word earns its place. The system speaks in facts and evidence, not promises. When it recommends an action, it shows its reasoning. When it asks for approval, it shows the evidence bundle. No hand-holding, no "AI assistant" energy — this is infrastructure, not a chatbot.

Emotional goals: "This is from the future." The first time someone sees their business as a live, explorable, predictive model — that's the moment. Wonder + paradigm shift + immediate trust that the system is real.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Refined density. Bloomberg Terminal's information architecture meets Figma's craft meets Stripe's elegance. Dark mode primary. The interface should feel like a command center for a new kind of company — calm authority, total situational awareness, zero clutter.

**References:**
- Figma + Linear — clean, fast, keyboard-driven, designed by people who care about craft
- Bloomberg Terminal — dense, information-rich, designed for power users
- Palantir Foundry — serious, analytical, handles complexity without dumbing it down

**Anti-references (NEVER):**
- Generic SaaS dashboards (no Notion/Linear clones, no card grids with metrics)
- AI slop (no purple-blue gradients, no glowing neon, no chat bubbles, no robot imagery)
- Enterprise bloatware (no Salesforce/SAP energy, no tabs-within-tabs, no enterprise gray)
- Warm/friendly aesthetics (the current terracotta/cream palette is being retired)

**Theme:** Dark mode dominant. Deep charcoal with subtle cool undertones (not pure black). Light mode exists but is secondary.

**Colors:** Muted, precise palette. One sharp accent used sparingly for primary actions. Semantic colors for data: blue=information, amber=attention-needed, red=blocked/critical, green=healthy/autonomous, purple=predictions/estimates. Estimated/inferred values visually distinct from observed values.

**Typography:** Technical but refined. No serifs in the product dashboard — this isn't a blog. Monospace for IDs, amounts, timestamps, code. Variable-width sans-serif for everything else. Clear hierarchy through weight and size, not decoration.

### Design Principles

1. **Information density over whitespace** — Every pixel earns its place. Data tables over card grids. Compact layouts over spacious ones. Users are power operators, not casual browsers.

2. **Evidence over assertion** — Show confidence levels on predictions. Show authority chains on actions. Show calibration scores on models. The interface constantly reinforces that the system KNOWS what it knows and KNOWS what it doesn't.

3. **State over decoration** — Color and visual weight communicate system state, not brand personality. Green means autonomous. Red means blocked. Purple means predicted. The design serves the data, not the other way around.

4. **Progressive complexity** — The overview is simple (3 health indicators). The drill-down is dense (full object history with timeline). Complexity reveals on demand, never overwhelms by default.

5. **Keyboard-first, mouse-friendly** — Command palette (Cmd+K) for everything. Number keys for navigation. Vim-style shortcuts for power users. But every action is also clickable.
