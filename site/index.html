How it works

1

Describe the work

Tell Nooterra what you need in plain English. It infers the tools, rules, and schedule automatically.

2

Review the charter

Every worker gets a charter: what it can do autonomously, what needs your approval, and what it must never do.

3

Deploy and supervise

Workers run 24/7. You approve sensitive actions, review logs, and track costs from one screen.

Built for work that matters

Guardrails that are actually enforced

Not prompt instructions. Runtime-enforced action classification. Workers cannot bypass their charter.

canDo: Read emails, Search the web askFirst: Issue refunds, Send to customers neverDo: Share data between customers

Approvals built in

Sensitive actions pause execution and route to you with full context. Approve from terminal, Slack, or webhooks.

Refund $49 for customer sarah@acme.com Source: Stripe charge ch_3x...duplicate Policy: refunds require approval [approve] [edit] [reject]

Runs 24/7

Background daemon with crash recovery, cron scheduling, webhook triggers, and auto-start on login.

$ nooterra daemon start $ nooterra dashboard Workers: 3 active, 1 paused Requests today: 47 handled Pending approvals: 2

Any AI provider

ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, OpenRouter, Groq, or local models via Ollama. Swap anytime without losing worker identity.

$ nooterra > /provider anthropic Provider set to Anthropic (Claude). Workers keep their charter, knowledge, and execution history.
